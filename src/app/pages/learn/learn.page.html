Rockets

Used to escape earth atmosphere and propulse a probe to the desired altitude / velocity

The vehicle used depends on the weight of the payload and the desired altitude for the mission.

Examples:

The Space Shuttle launched Hubble Telescope

The Delta 2 rocket launched NOAA satellites

What is an orbit?

Orbit: Curved path (circular or elliptical) around another object

Gravity: Cause of attraction of bodies.

Angular Momentum: Force acquired with speed around a body

Given enough momentum, the two forces balance, resulting in an orbital path.

LEO

Low Earth Orbit: 160 to 1000 km altitude.

Typically: Earth imaging and weather satellites.

The International Space station at 400 km.

The Hubble Space telescope at 555 km.



MEO

Medium Earth Orbits: Between LEO and GEO

Typically: Global positioning systems (GPS)

Galileo satellites Constellation to provide large coverage.



GEO

Geostationary orbit: Follows the equator at 35 700km

Maintain relative position over one particular area

Typically: Communication satellites
